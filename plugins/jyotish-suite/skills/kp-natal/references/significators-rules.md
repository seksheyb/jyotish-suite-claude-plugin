# KP Significators — Rules

## Definition
A planet is a "significator" of a house if it has the power to deliver matters of that house when its dasha runs.

## The 4-level significator framework (in order of strength)

### Level 1 (Strongest) — Star occupant of the house
Planets sitting in the star of the planet that occupies the house.

Example: If Sun is in the 10th house, and Mars is in a star of which Sun is the lord (Krittika, Uttara Phalguni, or Uttara Ashadha), then Mars is an L1 significator of the 10th house.

### Level 2 — Occupants of the house
Planets physically sitting in the house.

Example: Sun in 10th = L2 significator of 10th.

### Level 3 — Star occupants of the house-lord
Planets sitting in the star of the planet that **rules** the house cusp.

Example: 10th cusp in Capricorn → 10th lord = Saturn. Any planet in star of Saturn (Pushya, Anuradha, Uttara Bhadrapada) = L3 significator of 10th.

### Level 4 (Weakest) — House-lord itself
The lord of the cusp.

Example: 10th cusp in Capricorn → Saturn = L4 significator of 10th.

## Special additions

### Rahu/Ketu signification
Rahu and Ketu signify the houses they occupy AND the houses signified by:
- The lord of the sign they occupy (their "depositor")
- Any planet conjunct them (within ~3° in same sign)

If Rahu in Aquarius (Saturn's sign) is conjunct Mercury — Rahu signifies whatever Saturn signifies + whatever Mercury signifies.

### Conjunctions with nodes
A planet conjunct Rahu or Ketu inherits the nodal "amplification" — its signification is intensified for both its own houses AND any houses signified by the conjunct node.

## Fruitful vs barren significators

Not every significator can deliver. A significator is **fruitful** when:
- It signifies the positive house combination AND
- Its sub-lord ALSO signifies the positive house combination

A significator is **barren** when:
- Its sub-lord signifies the negative set (e.g. for marriage, sub-lord signifies 6, 1, or 10)

A barren significator's dasha will run, but the matter won't fructify in that period — even if the planet itself is in the right star and house.

This is the most subtle and important rule in KP. Many predictions go wrong when astrologers identify significators correctly but fail to check the sub-lord level.

## Practical workflow for listing significators of a house

1. Identify the cusp of the house (its degree)
2. Identify the cusp's sign-lord (= house-lord)
3. List the house's occupants (planets within the cusp's degree range — between this cusp and the next cusp)
4. For each occupant, identify its star-lord
5. List all planets in the star of any occupant → **L1**
6. List the occupants → **L2**
7. List all planets in the star of the house-lord → **L3**
8. The house-lord itself → **L4**
9. Add Rahu/Ketu if they're in this house, or conjunct any planet in this house, or in star of an occupant
10. For each significator listed, note its sub-lord and check whether sub-lord is in positive set → fruitful, or in negative set → barren

## Cross-reference with chart

Most KP-output software provides a pre-computed significator table. Use it as primary, but **verify by deriving Level 1 yourself** for the relevant houses — software bugs do occur, especially around node conjunctions.

If chart only provides "Planet → Houses signified" reverse view, derive house → planet view by inverting.

## Common errors

- Listing only L4 (house-lord) — too weak
- Forgetting L1 (star-occupants) — strongest
- Skipping the sub-lord fruitful/barren check
- Including outer planets in significator list (KP is 9-graha)
- Treating combust planets as full significators (they're weakened)
- Forgetting node conjunction rules
