# Teva (Chart Type) Classification — Lal Kitab

Teva is the chart's **archetypal pattern**. It tells you what kind of life this chart is built to live, before you read any individual house. Always assign one **dominant teva** + up to two **secondary teva** modifiers.

Classification is by planetary distribution patterns, not by single configurations. A chart can have multiple teva traits — name the strongest first.

---

## 1. Andha Teva (Blind Chart)

### Triggers
- Both luminaries (Sun and Moon) afflicted (debilitated, sleeping, or with Saturn/Rahu/Ketu)
- AND houses 1, 4, 7, 10 (kendras) at least 2 of 4 are empty

### Pattern
Native operates without inner clarity. Major life decisions made reactively. Direction-finding is the lifelong challenge — not capability, but knowing *what to aim at*.

### Life themes
- Multiple career pivots, none feeling right
- Drift between cities / partners / paths
- Insight comes very late, often after major losses
- Strong charts in this category produce mystics and breakthrough thinkers when they finally find direction; weak charts stay lost

### Farman reference
Farman 36, Vol 5 (1952)

---

## 2. Lula Teva (Lame Chart)

### Triggers
- Mars and Saturn in mutual aspect or conjunction
- AND one of them in 1, 4, or 8

### Pattern
Movement / livelihood / physical capacity is structurally constrained. The native carries a specific burden that limits a key faculty.

### Life themes
- Health issues affecting mobility, posture, joints
- Career advancement that requires more effort than peers' for same result
- Often forced into entrepreneurial / self-directed paths because conventional ones fail

### Variants
- **Mars-led Lula** — physical / surgical / accident-based limitations
- **Saturn-led Lula** — slow attrition, chronic pain, structural delays

### Farman reference
Farman 39, Vol 5 (1952)

---

## 3. Behra Teva (Deaf Chart)

### Triggers
- Mercury debilitated (in 12) OR sleeping
- AND 3rd house compromised (Saturn or Ketu in 3, or 3rd lord afflicted)

### Pattern
Communication / signal-reception is impaired. The native may speak but not be heard, or hear but not register accurately.

### Life themes
- Misunderstandings dominate relationships
- Sales / persuasion / influence careers underperform
- Information arrives late
- Often discover their voice through writing rather than speaking

### Farman reference
Farman 41, Vol 5 (1952)

---

## 4. Sukhi Teva (Happy Chart)

### Triggers
- 2 or more planets in their pakka ghar
- AND no severe rin (max 1 mild rin)
- AND luminaries (Sun, Moon) at minimum neutral, ideally exalted or in pakka ghar

### Pattern
Default ease. Things go right without elaborate effort. The native's challenge is usually **purpose** — not how to win, but what to win at.

### Life themes
- Comfortable family backgrounds
- Material adequacy from early on
- Relationships work without intensive maintenance
- Risk: complacency, avoidance of growth

### Farman reference
Farman 35, Vol 5 (1952)

---

## 5. Dukhi Teva (Sorrowful Chart)

### Triggers
- 2 or more rins active (any combination)
- AND 2 or more sleeping planets
- AND at least one luminary debilitated or buried

### Pattern
Compounding hardship across multiple life domains. Suffering is not random — it's structural and follows lineage patterns.

### Life themes
- Multiple recurring losses
- Health, wealth, and relationships all carry friction
- Strong upaay discipline can cut this in half within 3–6 years
- Without upaay, chart tends to repeat the same patterns through different surfaces

### Farman reference
Farman 38, Vol 5 (1952)

---

## 6. Rajyogi Teva (Royal Chart)

### Triggers (very specific — not a generic "raja yoga")
- Sun in pakka ghar (1) OR exalted (1) AND aspected by Jupiter
- AND Saturn in pakka ghar (10) OR exalted (7)
- AND no severe rin

### Pattern
Authority, command, and visibility are natural. Whether material or spiritual, the native operates from position rather than petition.

### Life themes
- Leadership roles arrive earlier than peers'
- Public visibility / following
- Often have one major Saturn-year crisis (36 or 42) that tests but doesn't dethrone

### Farman reference
Farman 34, Vol 5 (1952)

### Caution
Lal Kitab Rajyogi Teva is **not** the same as Parashari Raja Yoga. Don't confuse them. Many Parashari Raja Yogas don't qualify as Lal Kitab Rajyogi Teva.

---

## 7. Pujari Teva (Spiritual / Priest Chart)

### Triggers
- Jupiter in 9 (pakka ghar)
- AND Ketu in 9 OR 12 OR aspecting Jupiter
- AND Saturn supportive (pakka ghar 10, or in 7 exalted)

### Pattern
The chart is built for spiritual practice / teaching / service-oriented work. Material ambition feels off-key for these natives even when they pursue it.

### Life themes
- Drawn to teaching, healing, religious / contemplative work
- Material life often "good enough" rather than abundant — the chart prioritizes meaning
- Can fail badly if forced into pure-commerce roles

### Farman reference
Farman 37, Vol 5 (1952)

---

## 8. Mishra Teva (Mixed / Composite)

When the chart shows traits of 2+ teva but doesn't qualify cleanly for any, classify as Mishra and name the closest two. Most modern charts are Mishra.

Output format:
```
Dominant teva: Mishra (Sukhi-Dukhi)
  • Sukhi traits: [which ones present]
  • Dukhi traits: [which ones present]
  • Net: [which side is stronger]
```

## How Teva Affects the Reading

Teva is **not just descriptive** — it changes upaay priority:

| Teva | Upaay Priority |
|------|----------------|
| Andha | Awakening sleeping planets first; clarity-bringing remedies (Sun + Jupiter) |
| Lula | Mars-Saturn pacification before any other rin |
| Behra | Mercury strengthening; 3rd house upaay |
| Sukhi | Maintenance only; avoid disruption |
| Dukhi | Lineage rin upaay (Pitri + Matri) take absolute priority |
| Rajyogi | Sun-Saturn maintenance; Saturn-year preparation upaay |
| Pujari | Avoid material-amplification upaay; reinforce Jupiter-Ketu |
| Mishra | Address the more dominant teva first |

State the teva-driven upaay priority in Phase 9 output.
