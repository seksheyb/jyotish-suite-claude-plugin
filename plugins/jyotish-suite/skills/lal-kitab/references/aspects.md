# Lal Kitab Aspects (Drishti)

Lal Kitab aspects are **fundamentally different** from Parashari aspects. Do not import the 7th-house universal aspect or the special Mars/Jupiter/Saturn aspects. Use only what's below.

---

## Core Principle

Each planet aspects specific houses *from* its own position, and the aspect can be **direct (seedhi)** or **gaze (nazar)**. Direct aspects carry full effect; gaze aspects are weaker and conditional.

---

## Aspect Table

| Planet  | Aspects Houses (counted from itself, inclusive) |
|---------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Sun     | 7th                                              |
| Moon    | 7th                                              |
| Mars    | 4th, 7th, 8th                                    |
| Mercury | 7th                                              |
| Jupiter | 5th, 7th, 9th                                    |
| Venus   | 7th                                              |
| Saturn  | 3rd, 7th, 10th                                   |
| Rahu    | 5th, 7th, 9th, 12th                              |
| Ketu    | 5th, 7th, 9th (some Farmans add 2nd)            |

Counted **inclusive** of the planet's own house. So Saturn in house 1 aspects houses 3, 7, 10 (counting 1 as 1, 2, 3 = aspect on 3; 1 through 7 = aspect on 7; 1 through 10 = aspect on 10).

---

## Mutual Aspect Rules

When two planets aspect each other, the **enemy/friend relationship** determines outcome:

- **Friend-aspecting-friend** → results enhance, both planets give better
- **Enemy-aspecting-enemy** → results spoil, both planets damaged
- **Friend aspecting enemy** → friend tries to help; enemy may receive but distorts
- **Neutral aspect** → modulates current house but doesn't change planet's intrinsic strength

Use the friendship table from `pakka_ghar.md` §5 to evaluate.

---

## Aspect-Triggered Awakening

A **sleeping planet** (no conjunction, no aspect on it) is dead. It awakens when:
1. Another planet aspects it per the table above
2. A transiting planet aspects it during its varshphal year
3. An upaay specifically targets it

A natal aspect on a sleeping planet does **not** count as awakening if both planets are in mutual aspect-isolation from the rest of the chart — they form a "dead pair" and require external intervention.

## Special Aspect Rules

### Saturn's Aspect on Sun
Wherever this occurs, it triggers **Pitri Rin signature**. Do not skip — flag in rin diagnosis.

### Rahu's Aspect on Moon
Triggers **Matri Rin signature** if Moon is also in 4 or 8.

### Saturn's Aspect on Venus
Marriage delay or Stri Rin indicator. Especially severe if Venus is in 6th (debilitated).

### Jupiter's 5th-house Aspect
Universally protective. Mitigates rin severity by one tier wherever it lands.

### Rahu's 12th-house Aspect
Rahu is the only planet with a 12th-house aspect in Lal Kitab — this represents **karmic baggage transmitted to the house Rahu sits in plus 12 houses ahead**. Major diagnostic for ancestral debt patterns.

## Houses That Never Give Aspect Relief

Even if a benefic aspects them, these positions remain compromised without upaay:
- Sun in 7 (debilitated)
- Moon in 8 (debilitated)
- Mars in 4 (debilitated)
- Saturn in 1 (debilitated)
- Venus in 6 (debilitated)
- Mercury in 12 (debilitated)

Aspects can soften these positions but not redeem them. Only pakka ghar of the lord of the affliction-house can truly cancel.

## How to Apply (Procedural)

For every planet in the chart:
1. Look up its house position
2. From the aspect table, find which houses it aspects
3. List which planets sit in those aspected houses
4. Note whether each aspect is friendly, enemy, or neutral
5. For sleeping-planet check: a planet has *zero* aspects ON it from the table = sleeping

Build the aspect map before Phase 4 (sleeping check) and keep it for use in Phase 5 (rin) and Phase 8A (house reading).
