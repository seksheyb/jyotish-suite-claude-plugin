# Upaay (Remedies) Catalog — Lal Kitab

Lal Kitab's distinguishing feature: **physical, action-based remedies**. Not mantras. Not pujas. Not gems (with rare exceptions). Specific actions performed in specific ways at specific times.

Every upaay below cites a Farman. Modern adaptations are flagged. Each upaay states **for whom**, **what to do**, **timing**, and **caution**.

## 1. Pitri Rin Upaay

### 1.1 — Flowing Water + Coconut + Almonds (Primary)
- **For:** Pitri Rin (any trigger), especially Sun-Saturn or Sun-Rahu affliction
- **Action:** On any *amavasya* (new moon), float a coconut + 100g of almonds (badam) in flowing water (river, canal — not stagnant pond). Do not turn back while walking away.
- **Frequency:** Every amavasya for 6 months minimum; extend to 12 months for severe cases
- **Caution:** Never use stagnant water. Never look back after offering.
- **Farman:** Farman 8, Vol 2 (1940)

### 1.2 — Wheat to Crows
- **For:** Pitri Rin, especially when 9th house is empty or Sun is buried
- **Action:** Daily, before eating, place 100g of wheat flour kneaded into balls (with a few drops of water and a pinch of jaggery) for crows. Place on a rooftop or open ground.
- **Frequency:** Daily for 90 days minimum
- **Caution:** Must be performed by the native personally, not by a delegate
- **Farman:** Farman 22, Vol 3 (1941)

### 1.3 — Gold Ear-Piercing for Sons
- **For:** Pitri Rin affecting sons (5th house affliction)
- **Action:** If the native has sons, ensure each son's ears are pierced with gold (specifically gold, not silver) before age 8. For native himself, wearing a small gold earring in right ear (men) helps strengthen Sun.
- **Frequency:** One-time
- **Caution:** Has cultural / contemporary implications; recommend only if native is open to it. Modern adaptation: small gold object kept on person daily.
- **Farman:** Farman 16, Vol 4 (1942) + modern adaptation noted

### 1.4 — Donating Wheat at Religious Site
- **For:** Pitri Rin compounded with sleeping Sun
- **Action:** Donate raw wheat (5 kg) at a temple or gurudwara on Sunday morning
- **Frequency:** Once per month for 11 months
- **Caution:** Donate without seeking acknowledgment
- **Farman:** Farman 8, Vol 2 (1940)

### 1.5 — Sun Worship Ritual (Restricted)
- **For:** Pitri Rin with Sun in 7 (debilitated)
- **Action:** Offer water from a copper vessel to the rising sun daily (within first hour of sunrise). Mix a pinch of red sandalwood (raktachandan) and a few grains of red rice. Pour while standing.
- **Frequency:** Daily for 1 year, ideally lifelong
- **Caution:** Do **not** combine with #1.1 on the same morning
- **Farman:** Farman 12, Vol 1 (1939)

## 2. Matri Rin Upaay

### 2.1 — Silver Square (Primary)
- **For:** Matri Rin (any trigger), especially Moon-Saturn or Moon-Ketu
- **Action:** Bury a small solid silver square (approx 1 inch × 1 inch, minimum 10g) in the foundation of the native's home, ideally in the north-east corner. If burying is impossible, keep it in the puja-place / a clean drawer in the bedroom.
- **Frequency:** One-time, but check yearly that it remains in place
- **Caution:** Must be solid silver, not silver-plated. Must not be sold or removed without ritual replacement.
- **Farman:** Farman 5, Vol 2 (1940)

### 2.2 — Milk to Children
- **For:** Matri Rin compounded with 4th-house affliction
- **Action:** Distribute fresh milk (not packaged) to children under 8 years of age — not your own. 5–10 children minimum.
- **Frequency:** Every Monday for 11 months
- **Caution:** Do not announce or take credit for the act
- **Farman:** Farman 9, Vol 2 (1940)

### 2.3 — Water Vessel at Cremation Ground
- **For:** Severe Matri Rin (Moon buried by Rahu, or Moon in 8 + afflicted)
- **Action:** Place a clay pot (matka) full of water at a cremation ground / shamshan ghat. Walk away without turning back.
- **Frequency:** Once per year for 3 years on the native's birthday
- **Caution:** This is a heavy upaay — only for severe cases. Female natives may delegate this to a male family member if culturally appropriate.
- **Farman:** Farman 18, Vol 4 (1942)

### 2.4 — White Items in Mother's Use
- **For:** Matri Rin where mother is alive and suffering
- **Action:** Native gifts mother white-colored items: white sari/clothing, silver utensils, white sweets. Mother uses them. The native does not consume rice on the day of giving.
- **Frequency:** Quarterly, ongoing
- **Caution:** Only meaningful if mother is alive; if deceased, substitute with #2.3
- **Farman:** Farman 19, Vol 3 (1941)

### 2.5 — Avoid Selling Cow's Milk
- **For:** Matri Rin maintenance
- **Action:** Native must never engage in milk-trade business or sell dairy commercially. Consuming and gifting is fine.
- **Frequency:** Lifetime restriction
- **Caution:** Hard rule for these natives
- **Farman:** Farman 5, Vol 2 (1940)

## 3. Stri Rin Upaay

**Framing note:** When reading for female natives or non-traditional contexts, interpret these upaay as addressing the **feminine partnership principle**, not literally "wife." Apply the action; the result is the same.

### 3.1 — Donating Items in Mother-in-Law's Name
- **For:** Stri Rin where 7th house is afflicted by Saturn
- **Action:** Donate clothing or food in the name of one's mother-in-law (alive or deceased) on Fridays. Donations should be to elderly women.
- **Frequency:** Every Friday for 7 weeks, then quarterly maintenance
- **Caution:** Must be in the mother-in-law's name specifically; the act doesn't work if generalized
- **Farman:** Farman 11, Vol 2 (1940)

### 3.2 — Cow Donation / Cow Care
- **For:** Severe Stri Rin (Venus in 6 debilitated, or Venus + Rahu)
- **Action:** Sponsor full or partial care of a cow at a goshala (cow shelter) for at least 6 months. Or, if circumstances allow, gift a cow.
- **Frequency:** One-time gift, OR 6-month minimum sponsorship
- **Caution:** Must be a healthy, milking cow — not a sick or dying one
- **Farman:** Farman 25, Vol 3 (1941)

### 3.3 — Avoid Cremation Grounds Until Marriage
- **For:** Pre-marriage Stri Rin signature in male native
- **Action:** Native does not visit shamshan ghat / attend funerals until married. Substitute by sending donation in lieu of attendance.
- **Frequency:** Until marriage
- **Caution:** Difficult in close-family deaths — break only if absolutely necessary, then perform #3.1 immediately after
- **Farman:** Farman 21, Vol 4 (1942)

### 3.4 — White Flowers at Home
- **For:** Stri Rin maintenance during marriage
- **Action:** Keep fresh white flowers (jasmine, white rose, or any white flower) in a clean glass with water in the bedroom. Replace before they wilt.
- **Frequency:** Continuous, while married
- **Caution:** Do not let wilted flowers stay — that compounds the problem
- **Farman:** Farman 11, Vol 2 (1940)

### 3.5 — No Black Clothing on Fridays
- **For:** All Stri Rin natives
- **Action:** Never wear pure black on Fridays. White / cream / pastels preferred.
- **Frequency:** Lifetime
- **Caution:** Hard rule
- **Farman:** Modern Tradition (post-1970s clarification) — flag as not from original Farmans but widely practiced

## 4. Kanya Rin Upaay

### 4.1 — Help in Daughter's Marriage
- **For:** Kanya Rin signature
- **Action:** Contribute financially or organizationally to the marriage of a daughter not your own (relative, neighbor, community member). The contribution should be meaningful, not token.
- **Frequency:** One-time per case; repeat for each Kanya Rin trigger
- **Caution:** Must be voluntary and undisclosed
- **Farman:** Farman 23, Vol 3 (1941)

### 4.2 — Green-Items Restriction
- **For:** Kanya Rin from afflicted Mercury
- **Action:** Avoid commercial trade in green items (green vegetables specifically, green clothing). Personal use is fine.
- **Frequency:** Lifetime
- **Caution:** Read narrowly — this is about *trade*, not about consumption or gifts
- **Farman:** Farman 13, Vol 2 (1940)

### 4.3 — Gifts to Young Girls
- **For:** Kanya Rin general remediation
- **Action:** Gift small items (sweets, school supplies, clothing) to young girls (under 12) on Wednesdays. 5–10 girls per occasion.
- **Frequency:** Monthly for 11 months
- **Caution:** Must be modest gifts and culturally appropriate; do not photograph or publicize
- **Farman:** Farman 24, Vol 3 (1941)

## 5. Bhratra Rin Upaay

### 5.1 — Honey + Sweet Donation
- **For:** Bhratra Rin (Mars-Saturn / Mars-Rahu affliction)
- **Action:** Donate sealed honey (1 kg minimum) and besan-laddu (or any pure-ghee sweet) at a temple on Tuesdays.
- **Frequency:** Every Tuesday for 7 weeks, then quarterly
- **Caution:** Donate in elder brother's name if applicable, regardless of relationship status
- **Farman:** Farman 7, Vol 2 (1940)

### 5.2 — Red Items Restriction
- **For:** Bhratra Rin with debilitated Mars (Mars in 4)
- **Action:** Avoid wearing pure red on Saturdays. Red on other days (especially Tuesday) is fine.
- **Frequency:** Lifetime
- **Caution:** Hard rule
- **Farman:** Farman 15, Vol 1 (1939)

### 5.3 — Maintaining Brother Relationship
- **For:** Active Bhratra Rin
- **Action:** Make conscious effort to maintain contact with elder brother (or his family if deceased). Gift on his birthday and on Bhai Dooj. Do not let estrangement persist.
- **Frequency:** Ongoing
- **Caution:** If estrangement is structural, contribute to brother's welfare anonymously
- **Farman:** Farman 20, Vol 3 (1941)

## 6. Self / Atma Rin Upaay

### 6.1 — Yellow / Saffron Cloth Practice
- **For:** Self Rin from afflicted Jupiter
- **Action:** Wear a yellow or saffron thread / small cloth on the body daily. Or keep saffron in the temple/puja-area at home.
- **Frequency:** Continuous, lifetime
- **Caution:** Replace when soiled — don't continue wearing degraded thread
- **Farman:** Farman 4, Vol 2 (1940)

### 6.2 — Service in Religious / Spiritual Place
- **For:** Self Rin with Jupiter and Ketu both sleeping
- **Action:** Volunteer time (not just donation) at a temple, gurudwara, ashram, or spiritual organization. Physical service preferred over administrative.
- **Frequency:** Minimum 1 day per month for 1 year, ideally ongoing
- **Caution:** Do not seek positions of authority within the institution
- **Farman:** Farman 33, Vol 5 (1952)

### 6.3 — Vegetarian Restriction on Thursdays
- **For:** Self Rin baseline
- **Action:** Strictly vegetarian on Thursdays. No alcohol on Thursdays.
- **Frequency:** Lifetime
- **Caution:** Hard rule
- **Farman:** Modern Tradition (clarification of Farman 4, Vol 2 (1940)) — flagged

### 6.4 — Daughter / Niece Care
- **For:** Self Rin from "blind chart" pattern
- **Action:** Take active responsibility in education or wellbeing of a daughter or niece (own or relative's). Long-term commitment.
- **Frequency:** Multi-year commitment
- **Caution:** Must be sustained, not one-time
- **Farman:** Farman 32, Vol 5 (1952)

## 8. Sleeping Planet Awakening Upaay

For each sleeping planet, run the Phase 7 strengthening upaay PLUS:

### 8.1 — Conjunction-Simulation
- **Action:** Wear / carry an item associated with a friendly planet of the sleeping one. Example: if Jupiter is sleeping, wear yellow + carry a piece of pure turmeric root in pocket — Sun (friend of Jupiter) symbolically wakes Jupiter.
- **Caution:** Use friendship table from `pakka_ghar.md` §5 to choose friend planet correctly

### 8.2 — Activation Through Karaka Action
- **Action:** Engage in the natural activity of the sleeping planet. Sleeping Mars → take up physical activity / sport. Sleeping Mercury → start writing / commerce. Sleeping Venus → spend on aesthetic improvements at home.
- **Frequency:** Sustained until the planet's varshphal year activates

### 8.3 — Keeping Planet's Color in Field of Vision
- **Action:** Keep the planet's signature color in daily-use items (bag, towel, room curtains).
- **Color map:**
    - Sun: orange / saffron
    - Moon: white / cream
    - Mars: red
    - Mercury: green
    - Jupiter: yellow
    - Venus: white / pink
    - Saturn: dark blue / black (use sparingly)
    - Rahu: indigo / smoke gray
    - Ketu: multi-colored / brown

## 9. Year-Specific Varshphal Upaay

For the danger years (21, 36, 42, 48, 54, 63 — matching `timing.md` Filter 3
and `varshphal.md`'s Major Year Markers, both of which include age 54's
Mars+Saturn health watch), perform the year-ruler's strengthening upaay PLUS:

### 9.1 — Age 36 (First Saturn Year)
- Add: Donate iron items (small iron utensils, iron nails) on Saturdays
- Restrict: Avoid major financial commitments in months 7–9 of the year
- Farman: F.10, V.2 + F.36, V.5

### 9.2 — Age 42 (Saturn + Rahu — most dangerous year)
- Combined Saturn + Rahu protocol:
  - Donate mustard oil weekly throughout the year
  - Float coal in water on amavasyas
  - Avoid alcohol entirely for the full year
  - Avoid risky travel in months 4–6 of the year
- Farman: F.42, V.5

### 9.3 — Age 48 (Third Saturn Year)
- Career-consolidation rather than expansion
- Repeat Age 36 upaay
- Add: Visit elderly parents / parental graves; honor lineage actively
- Farman: F.48, V.5

### 9.4 — Age 63 (Rahu Major)
- Spiritual practice intensifies
- Add: Daily reading of a chosen sacred text (15+ minutes)
- Restrict: Avoid taking on new business ventures
- Farman: F.63, V.5

### 9.5 — Age 54 (Mars + Saturn — Health Watch)
- Combined Mars + Saturn protocol:
  - Donate honey + sealed sweets on Tuesdays (Mars) AND mustard oil on
    Saturdays (Saturn) — do not combine on the same day (see §11 conflict rules)
  - Schedule a full health check-up (surgical/structural risk emphasis) in
    the months surrounding the birthday
  - Avoid strenuous physical risk-taking in months 4–6 of the year
- Farman: F.7, V.2 + F.10, V.2 + F.54, V.5

## 10. Universal Maintenance Upaay

For all natives — light practices that build baseline chart strength:

1. **Charity on birthday** — donate something every birthday (any planet, any item). Lal Kitab considers birthday-charity baseline maintenance.
2. **Cleanliness in north-east corner of home** — never store junk, broken items, or shoes in the NE corner.
3. **No shoe-on-shoe stacking** — keep shoes flat-side up, never stacked sole-up
4. **Don't sleep with feet pointing north** — affects Pitri energies
5. **Keep a small water vessel near bed** — empty in morning, refill at night
6. **Respect to elders** — Lal Kitab considers disrespect to elders as actively damaging Sun/Saturn

These are universal; recommend regardless of specific findings.

## 11. Hard Rules and Cautions

### Conflict rules — DO NOT combine simultaneously:
- Sun strengthening (#1.5) + Saturn upaay (#7 Saturn) on same day
- Mars strengthening + Mercury strengthening on same day (enemies)
- Wearing red and black together at any time

### Pregnancy contraindications:
- #2.3 (cremation ground vessel) — never during pregnancy
- #3.3 (cremation ground avoidance) — extends through pregnancy
- #5.1 (sweet/honey donation) — fine during pregnancy

### Health contraindications:
- Diabetic natives — substitute jaggery for sugar in any sweet-donation upaay
- Allergic natives — substitute almonds/wheat with allergen-free alternatives, prefix the substitution by stating intent

### Children performing upaay:
- Children under 12 should not perform upaay independently. Parent performs on child's behalf with intention stated.

### Female natives:
- All upaay are valid for female natives. Where original Farmans specify male-default actions (e.g. ear-piercing), apply the spirit of the rule (gold on the right side of body).

### When upaay must STOP:
- If the configuration that triggered the rin shifts via transit (rare in natal Lal Kitab, but possible) — re-read the chart
- If the relative who triggered the rin (e.g. mother) has passed — some upaay shift to memorial form
- If three years of upaay show zero life-pattern shift — reconsider the diagnosis itself, not just intensify the upaay

### What upaay CANNOT do:
- Cancel a debilitation completely (only soften)
- Override a sleeping pakka-ghar planet (only awaken in moments)
- Predict specific dates of fructification
- Replace medical / legal / professional intervention for material problems

State this clearly when prescribing — manage expectations.

## Citation Format

Throughout the reading, cite Farmans as: `[Farman X, Vol Y (year)]` — e.g. `[Farman 8, Vol 2 (1940)]`.

If a remedy is from modern tradition (post-1970s clarification), cite as: `[Modern Tradition — clarification of Farman X, Vol Y]`.

Never cite a remedy without provenance. If you can't find a Farman for it, don't prescribe it.
