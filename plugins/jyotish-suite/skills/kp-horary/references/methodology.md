# KP Horary — Full Methodology

## Core principles

1. **The Sub Lord decides.** Krishnamurti's central rule. Sign-lord and star-lord set the field; the sub-lord delivers the verdict.

2. **249 system.** The zodiac's 360° is divided into 249 unequal segments based on Vimshottari proportions. Each segment is a unique combination of Sign + Nakshatra + Sub. The horary number 1-249 selects the Lagna's exact starting sub-division.

3. **Time of question matters for planets, not Lagna.** Planet positions, dasha, and Ruling Planets are computed for the moment the question is asked. The Lagna comes from the number, not from rising sign.

4. **House signification through stars.** A planet primarily signifies the houses owned/occupied by the planet in whose star it sits, secondarily the houses it itself owns/occupies. Star-lord effects > own effects.

5. **Cuspal Sub Lord (CSL) is paramount.** For every house, the sub-lord of its cusp determines whether matters of that house will fructify.

## Eight-step horary read

### Step 1 — Frame the question
Specific, time-bound, single-issue. Reject "will I be happy" or "what will my future be like." Accept "will I get the job at X within 3 months" or "will the property sale close before Diwali."

### Step 2 — Identify primary house
Map the question to its primary house and supporting houses (combinations table). Identify the negation set (houses whose activation would deny the matter).

### Step 3 — Examine the primary CSL
- What does its star-lord signify (occupation + ownership)?
- What does the sub-lord itself signify (occupation + ownership)?
- Does the chain include the positive set? The negative set?

**Rule:**
- CSL signifies positive set only → matter fructifies
- CSL signifies negative set only → matter denied
- CSL signifies both → look at sub-sub-lord; if SSL aligns with positive, fructifies; if with negative, denied
- CSL signifies neither → matter does not fructify in current dasha

### Step 4 — List significators of relevant houses
For each house in the positive combination, list:
- **Level 1:** Planets in the star of the planet occupying that house
- **Level 2:** Planets occupying that house
- **Level 3:** Planets in the star of the lord of that house
- **Level 4:** The lord of the house

Level 1 is strongest, Level 4 is weakest. Conjunction with Rahu/Ketu in a house adds those nodes to the significators.

### Step 5 — Compute Ruling Planets
Per `references/ruling-planets.md`. Show calculation.

### Step 6 — RP cross-check
The matter fructifies during the dasha of a planet that is **both a significator of the positive set AND a Ruling Planet**. If RP planets do not appear among significators, fructification is uncertain or delayed.

### Step 7 — Timing via DBA
- Current Mahadasha lord must signify positive set (or be in star of one that does)
- Bhukti lord must signify positive set
- Antara narrows to weeks; Sookshma narrows to days
- Transit of Jupiter/Sun through significator stars confirms

### Step 8 — Verdict
- Outcome (Y/N/conditional)
- Confidence (H/M/L)
- Window (specific dates)
- Caveats (sandhi, retrograde, sub-sub conflicts)
- Action

## Special rules

**Retrograde planets:** Give effects of the planet in whose star they sit, not their own. Exception: Rahu/Ketu always give effects of their dispositor and any conjunct planet.

**Combust planets (within 8.5° of Sun):** Lose strength as significators unless they are Ruling Planets.

**Planet at sandhi (within 0°30' of sign edge):** Cannot give clear results. CSL at sandhi = unreliable, ask question again later.

**Mrityu Bhaga, Pushkara, Gandanta degrees:** Note as flags but in horary they are secondary to CSL verdict.

**Empty houses:** Use the lord of the house and planets in its star.

**Multiple planets in one star:** All are significators; the closest in degree to the star-mid-point is strongest.

## Common failure modes to avoid

- Reading the chart before framing the question precisely
- Overweighting Jupiter or benefics — KP doesn't care about benefic/malefic, only signification
- Ignoring CSL when it contradicts visual planet placement
- Skipping the RP cross-check
- Forgetting to check sub-sub-lord when CSL is ambiguous
- Mixing horary with natal — keep them separate
